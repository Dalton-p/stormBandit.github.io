Goal #1 - Communication and Comprehension

  • Goal

    I would like to become better at communicating with others about systems and processes within the system. I find that when colleagues of mine are speaking in depth about how a system works I can become lost. As well when I try to explain myself to others I can confuse even myself in trying to explain how something I have developed works.

  • Action Plan

    Each day at Brock we do a scrum where the team gets together and we share what we were working on the previous day, what has us stuck, and any other questions we have that the team ma be able to answer. Scrum gives us all a good chance to catch up with eachother, learn about other parts of the project and ask questions to clarify misunderstandings. I will take full advantage of these scrum sessions in order to improve my oral communication.

  • Measure of Success

    The best way to measure whether or not I have become a better oral communicator is within those scrum sessions, when I explain my process to others and realize that they are following along and understand what I mean, then I will feel like I have achieved this goal. Although it is always something that can be improved.

  • Reflection

    One definitive way to know whether or not I have progessed on a goal during a work term is to hear my supervisor's thoughts on the goal. In this case, my talent manager told me that I have shown progress on this goal within my four months at Brock. He told me that I became much better at communicating through the online services such as Jira and Perforce and I was skilled in communicating during our daily scrums and during conference calls over skype. I feel that I did improve over the course of the term and became a better communicator accross all the platforms that we used at Brock.

Goal #2 - Understanding the Stack

  • Goal

    The system I am working with here at Brock is very large, sometimes it is extremely difficult to read the code and understand what is going on, this is a skill I would like to develop. It is important to be able to read old code, know how it works and what it is doing, to the be able to modify that code. This code is also very sparsely commented, so understanding the naming techniques of various classes and methods will help with this goal.

  • Action Plan

    The best way to get better at something is to practice. Reading and understanding old code is something we must do every time we are given a new task, so I believe there is plenty of time in this work term for me to practice and refine this skill.

  • Measure of Success

    This skill is one that will be difficut to measure, by the end of the term I should be familiar with the structure of the code and the conventions used, which will aid me in understanding the code. If I am still unable to read a function and not understand what it is used for then I will have not accomplished this goal.

  • Reflection

    In the beginning of my work term, the stack at Brock was very overwhelming, but as I worked through various parts of the stack on different projects the structure and layout of it all began to make more sense to me. With that I was much more easily able to navigate the source code and find what I was looking for in order to begin work. This skill is one that I will constantly be trying to improve upon, and I feel that these past four months were very beneficial in this aspect.

Goal #3 - Being a more Self-Sufficient Programmer

  • Goal

    One of the goals I am most ambitious to meet, but also may take the longest time to accomplish, is being more independant. I would like to be able to sit down, start a project and complete that project without needing aid from other people. I find that I am unable to do highly complex things without the help of another person that i can explain my questions to and they can answer them. It is a problem of not really knowing what I'm trying to do (or what the industry calls that thing) to be able to look it up myself.

  • Action Plan

    Expanding my knowledge of different processes, overtime will help me accomplish this goal. Reading more articles and books on computer programming will help.

  • Measure of Success

    I will have accompished this goal when I feel confident to do a project without having to message a colleague and ask "how do I do this" but instead I can do a quick internet search myself or figure it out in the code on my own.

  • Reflection

    This was another goal that I was very proud to hear my supervisor say, that I had made progress on. I also feel very accomplished in this goal, as I was able to figure a lot of things out on my own with very little help. Completing complicated tasks by myself makes me feel great, especially when I am able to do it in a time frame that sometimes surprised my supervisors.

Goal #5 - Expand my Repetoire of Software Tools

  • Goal

    In my position at Brock we use Windows as the main OS, Perforce as our main Version Control and JIRA as our task planner. These are all software packages that I have not used very much in the past, and I could learn a lot about at my time here.

  • Action Plan

    The knowledge i need to be successful when programming on a windows machine lies within my colleagues. I have already been shown so many different little trickt to make life easier on a Windows machine. Perforce and JIRA are tools that are very new to me and again my colleagues are probably the best resource for help on these, but as always the internet will also be a good place to find help.

  • Measure of Success

    I would like to be able to confidently explain, perhaps in an interview, the steps to using any of the software we use at Brock and why or how they would be an advantage over others of the same function.

  • Reflection

    This goal was probably the easiest to accomplish, I mean really all I had to do was show up. Learning Perforce was an interesting curve in the beginning, but I got the hang of it quite quickly. Jira was very similar to Phabricator which I have used previously. As for coding on a Windows machine, I learned all the commands and tricks that helped with my specific job quickly. Although we did not use the command prompt very often, which is something I would like to expand my knowledge on in the future.
