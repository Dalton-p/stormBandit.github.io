Official Title: Software Engineer

Description:
We are looking for a co-op Software Engineer to help us expand and improve our portfolio of products and services which are used by tens of millions of users per month. We have been very diligent about assembling a team of people who are passionate about creating amazing web products in an agile environment. This is an unbeatable opportunity for a student who wants to work at a digital media company with great people and the latest tech.

Expectations:
* Work on our internal network that serves billions of requests producing tens of millions of users monthly
* Utilize cutting edge technologies to combine relational database design methodologies with modern caching-based horizontally scaling systems
* Contribute to existing as well as build new systems using elastic and high availability methodologies primarily using php
* Excel in an agile environment overseeing multiple projects where the focus is getting the job done, not red tape

Essential Experience:
* We are language agnostic here so as long as you can code, you qualify
* Familiar with HTML, JavaScript, CSS, Ajax, jQuery
* Working knowledge of source control concepts using Git, SVN, TFS, CVS or equivalent
* Great verbal and interpersonal communications skills to work with others who are less technical
* Ability to perform multiple job tasks simultaneously

Job Perks:
* Money. Competitive hourly wage
* Fun. Yes, we offer the industry standard perks such as a fully stocked staff room complete with drinks, pool table, foosball, dart board, lounge area with TV and video games.
* Food. Participate in weekly off-site team building lunches
* Entertainment. Come out for full day social gathering "field trips" that occur every quarter.
* Environment. Work in a friendly, fun and innovative work environment with a multi-monitor workstation

My Experience at Adknown:
My experience with Adknown started out with the interview process, which very much enjoyed. After applying to the position I was promptly emailed an online test similar to many other companies in the same space. The in person interview was very well organized in my opinion and I had a lot of fun with it. The interview was conducted in a room with a whiteboard where I was encouraged to display my solutions to technical questions, and explain my thinking. After the first round of more theoretical technical questions, the interviewer handed me their laptop and told me that I was to use it during the next exercise to research any solutions to the problem they proposed. They began to go through a role playing scenario where I was acting as a developer trying to help a business man with their company website. This was a very interesting approach to a technical interview and gave me an opportunity to show that with a computer in hand I was able to find a solution to problems that may seem more difficult at first. The important part for a developer is that they can find a solution to a problem not memorize them all.

The orientation at Adknown was also very well organized, they came up with a way for the company and their coops to both benefit greatly during the onboarding process. They did this through creating a project idea for the coops to work on together that would eventually become an internal tool for the company to use in the future. In our case, we were building a Time Off Tracking Platform, where employees would be able to book time off for various reasons and they platform would handle reminding team members of absences ad well as requesting the approval from management. During the orientation period the team of coops worked together on the project and had a project manager dev assigned to the project to oversee and help teach. This was much better than simply giving us a tutorial to follow or pdf to read through. There were many opportunities during the project to use different technologies and learn new tools. During this process I was mainly responsible for creating the data model, building out a database schema, and creating the data access layer for the project. I also worked on setting up the nginx configuration and routing for the site.

Once the orientation project was over each of the coops was placed with a mentor, my mentor was mainly responsible for working on all the internal tools used by employees throughout the company to aid in completing their work. This was an incredible opportunity for me because there were several different projects that I was tasked with updating, fixing, adding to or replacing. This provided a wide range of technologies that I was able to work with, and continued to provide me with different challenges.

This term I was able to work with a variety of technologies, sharpening my skills with some familiar to me and being introduced to new ones. I spent quite a bit of time learning new, more indepth ways to use Docker and Docker Compose to host my local working environment. As well as honing my Php skills for both backend scripting and its powerful OOP capabilities. I had a lot of exposure to frontend programming as well, using HTML and javascript (jQuery and ajax) to accomplish complex tasks and improve the user experience.
